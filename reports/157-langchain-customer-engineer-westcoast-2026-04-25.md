**Why LangChain / Why Customer Engineer?**
"LangChain sits at the seam where my production work lives — RAG, agentic workflows, eval pipelines, schema-validated multi-agent systems. The Agentic Claims project I built (multi-agent pipeline with schema-validated JSON contracts to prevent cascading hallucinations) is the closest portfolio analog to LangGraph's pattern. I want to take that production discipline into customer enablement — building reference implementations, writing tutorials that survive model swaps, and educating enterprise teams on the failure modes that matter. Manga Lens (Chrome Web Store ship, 4 vision providers, 29 sites) is my proof that I can both build and document for other developers."

**One technical concept you've explained to a non-technical audience?**
"At Progress, clinical ops asked for 'fewer no-shows.' I had to translate the trade-off — false negatives in clinical risk are more expensive than false positives — into language clinical ops could act on. I shipped XGBoost with class weighting + threshold calibration + audit trail behind a FastAPI service, and wrote a system-limitation doc that framed precision/recall in clinical terms (which patients we'd over-flag vs miss). Adoption happened without over-claim. The lesson was: enablement is mostly limit-framing."
