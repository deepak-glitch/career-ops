At Progress Solutions I've built agentic LLM workflows for multi-step healthcare queries — eligibility checks, care workflow navigation — with >30% hallucination reduction and ~25% response stability gain via structured reasoning, tool discipline, and grounding rules. **Describe a time you operated like a CTO on a high-impact project.**
> The multi-agent healthcare claims pipeline at Progress Solutions started as a research prototype and I was the only engineer. I made the architectural call to use schema-validated JSON contracts between the 5 agents (Intake → Validation → Consistency → Duplicate → Risk Scoring) instead of free-form chain-of-thought. That meant designing the contracts, building the RAG-grounded CPT/ICD validation against vector-indexed policy docs, the ANN duplicate detection, the explainable risk scoring with audit-ready reasoning, and getting it to production with HIPAA-conscious governance. The output was reviewable end-to-end and cascading hallucinations were prevented. The CTO instinct here was to bias toward bounded contracts over agent autonomy — that's what made it production-grade.
